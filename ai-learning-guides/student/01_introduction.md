# Two Ways to Use AI — The Maxim

This excerpt summarizes Chapter 1 of the Student Guide. The full text is in
[`LearningWithAI_StudentGuide_v1.pdf`](LearningWithAI_StudentGuide_v1.pdf).

## The distinction

There are two fundamentally different ways a student can use an AI assistant:

1. **As a study aid**, where the AI helps the student learn a topic — by
   clarifying notation, suggesting counterexamples, generating alternative
   explanations, or producing practice problems
2. **As a homework completion engine**, where the AI produces an answer
   that the student submits

Both uses produce text. They look similar from the outside. But they have
opposite long-term effects: the first builds capability that compounds, the
second forms habits that erode it.

## Why the distinction matters

The student who uses AI as a study aid develops the ability to do mathematics
without AI — because the AI was a tool that helped them learn. The student
who uses AI as a completion engine becomes dependent on AI — because the
mathematics was never internalized; only the output was produced. After
several semesters of the second pattern, the student cannot do the work
even with the AI, because they have no framework for evaluating whether
the AI's output is right.

This is not a moral observation; it is an empirical one. The same student
under the two patterns will look identical at the moment of submission and
will have very different capabilities a year later.

## The maxim

> *Before you ask an AI to solve a problem, ask it something else first.*

The "something else" is what turns AI from a homework completion engine into
a study aid. The next two chapters of the Guide develop the operational
content of this maxim:

- **Six things AI does well** — when the maxim works ([`02_six_things_AI_does_well.md`](02_six_things_AI_does_well.md))
- **Six things AI does badly** — what to verify before trusting ([`03_six_things_AI_does_badly.md`](03_six_things_AI_does_badly.md))
- **The verification principle** — how to spot the difference ([`04_verification_principle.md`](04_verification_principle.md))

## Connection to the book's "verify, verify, verify, then trust" mantra

The book's Appendix D establishes the operational mantra: *verify, verify,
verify, then trust.* The Student Guide and the book's AI Audit boxes are two
sides of the same discipline. The mantra is what turns "AI output that
looks right" into "AI output that is right." The Guide develops the discipline
of verification at the level of study habits; the AI Audit boxes apply it to
specific epidemic-modeling failure modes.
